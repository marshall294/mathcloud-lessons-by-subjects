Year 5 Computing Lesson: Explore Scratch

Learning Objectives

  • Know the Scratch interface
  • Learn how to move sprites

What is Scratch?

Scratch is a simple computer programming tool that lets you create games, animations, and stories using blocks of code that you can snap together.

It’s like a puzzle—each block tells the computer what to do. Scratch helps you learn coding by letting you control characters called sprites.

Example: You can make a cat move across the screen, spin around, or say hello.

Activities/Tasks

  1. Open your web browser and go to scratch.mit.edu.
  2. Click on "Sign In" and log into your Scratch account.
  3. Click on "Create" to open a new project.
  4. Take a look at the Scratch interface:
    • Stage: where your sprites perform.
    • Blocks: colorful coding blocks to control your sprite.
    • Script area: where you build your program.
    • Sprite list: shows which characters are in your project.
  5. Select the Scratch Cat sprite (it's there by default).
  6. Click on the "Motion" blocks on the left side.
  7. Drag the move 10 steps block to the script area.
  8. Click it — the cat moves forward!
  9. Add more blocks to make your sprite do fun things:
    • Drag turn 15 degrees and attach it below the move block.
    • From "Control", add repeat 10 and put the other blocks inside it.
  10. Click the green flag to start the animation. Try changing numbers to make new movements!
