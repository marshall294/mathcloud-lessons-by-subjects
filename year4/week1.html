Year 4 Computing Lesson: What is Digital Publishing?

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what digital publishing is
  • Identify posters and newsletters as forms of digital publishing

What is Digital Publishing?

Digital Publishing means creating content (like posters, newsletters, or magazines) using a computer and sharing it online or printing it out.

Instead of drawing with pens or writing by hand, we use programs like Microsoft Word, Google Slides, or Publisher to design and organize our ideas.

Examples:

  • A school newsletter made in Microsoft Word
  • A birthday poster created using Google Slides
  • An online magazine about animals made with pictures and text

Activities/Tasks

  1. Talk together about what it means to publish something. Think about books, newspapers, and online articles.
  2. Learn that digital publishing uses computers to make posters, newsletters, or magazines.
  3. Look at real examples of posters and newsletters. Talk about what makes them look good and easy to read.
  4. In small groups, look at printed samples and answer:
    • What is this for?
    • Who is it for?
    • What makes it nice to look at?
  5. Each of you will plan your own poster:
    • Choose a topic you like (e.g., healthy eating, school rules, your favourite animal).
    • Use a blank template to add a title, some text, and at least one picture.
    • Think about colour, size, and where things go on your page.
  6. If you have time, start making your poster on a computer using Word, Publisher or Google Slides.

Resources

  • Printed examples of posters and newsletters
  • Computers or tablets (optional)
  • Blank poster templates (digital or printed)
